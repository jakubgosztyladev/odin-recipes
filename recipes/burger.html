Beef burger recipe

Description

This mouthwatering cheeseburger features a perfectly seasoned beef patty cooked to juicy perfection, topped with melted cheddar cheese that cascades over the edges. Nestled in a soft, sesame-studded brioche bun, it's layered with crisp lettuce, ripe tomato slices, and tangy pickles. The burger is complemented by a special sauce that adds a creamy, zesty kick to every bite.

Served alongside golden, crispy french fries and a refreshing glass of orange juice, this classic comfort meal is perfect for any occasion. Whether you're enjoying it as a weekend treat or sharing it with friends, this cheeseburger delivers that satisfying combination of flavors and textures that makes it an all-time favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb (450g) ground beef (80/20 blend)
  • 4 brioche burger buns with sesame seeds
  • 4 slices cheddar cheese
  • 4 lettuce leaves
  • 2 large tomatoes, sliced
  • 1 red onion, sliced into rings
  • Dill pickle slices
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter for cooking
  • Optional: bacon strips

For the Special Sauce:

  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 2 tablespoons ketchup
  • 1 tablespoon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon pickle relish
  • 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika

For Serving:

  • French fries
  • Fresh orange juice or your favorite beverage

Steps

  1. Prepare the special sauce: In a small bowl, mix together mayonnaise, ketchup, mustard, pickle relish, garlic powder, and paprika. Refrigerate until ready to use.
  2. Form the patties: Divide the ground beef into 4 equal portions (about 4 oz each). Gently shape into patties slightly larger than your buns, as they'll shrink during cooking. Make a small indentation in the center of each patty with your thumb to prevent bulging. Season both sides generously with salt and black pepper.
  3. Cook the patties: Heat a skillet or grill over medium-high heat. Add olive oil or butter. Cook the patties for 3-4 minutes on the first side without moving them. Flip and cook for another 3-4 minutes for medium doneness.
  4. Add the cheese: During the last minute of cooking, place a slice of cheddar cheese on each patty. Cover the pan or close the grill lid to help the cheese melt perfectly.
  5. Toast the buns: While the patties are resting, lightly toast the brioche buns on the grill or in a pan until golden brown.
  6. Assemble the burgers: Spread the special sauce on both halves of each bun. On the bottom bun, layer lettuce, tomato slices, the cheesy beef patty, pickles, and onion rings. Top with the upper bun.
  7. Serve: Plate your burgers alongside crispy french fries and a glass of fresh orange juice. Serve immediately while hot and enjoy!
